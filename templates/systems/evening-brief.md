# Evening Brief System

> **Depends on:** `systems/email-triage.md` (search methodology, priority tiers)

The evening brief is the heavy-lifting prep session. It sets {{USER_NAME}} up for the next day and makes sure nothing fell through the cracks today.

## When

End of day — run when {{USER_NAME}} is winding down, or automated at a set time.

## Contents

### 1. Loose Ends from Today
Surface anything unresolved that could bite them tomorrow:
- Emails that need a reply but haven't been sent
- Action items from today's meetings that need follow-up
- Anything someone is waiting on from {{USER_NAME}}
- Anything flagged as urgent that hasn't been addressed

### 2. Tomorrow's Calendar
- Full schedule with times and attendees
- Flag any conflicts or back-to-back crunches
- Note which meetings are in-person vs. virtual
- Call out if tomorrow is an office day or remote day

### 3. Meeting Prep Notes
For each important meeting tomorrow:
- Who's in the room and relevant context
- Purpose / agenda (if available)
- Recent emails or threads with attendees
- What decisions or inputs are needed from {{USER_NAME}}
- Suggested talking points if relevant

**External meetings get extra scrutiny.** Never summarize an external meeting from the calendar invite alone. Always pull email threads to understand the full backstory, stakes, and any unread materials. The calendar subject often undersells the importance.

### 4. Priority Messages to Address

Follow the full search methodology in `systems/email-triage.md`.

**Emails:**
- Run the full triage protocol — inbox pull, extend coverage back to morning (~8am), targeted sender searches as safety net
- Check Sent Items before flagging anything as "unanswered"
- Surface Tier 1 and 2 emails that haven't been handled

**Messages (Teams/Slack):**
- Surface priority DMs and group chats since this morning
- DMs first, then priority group chats

Always keep email and messaging in separate sections.

### 5. Advance Prep Radar
Scan the next 1-2 weeks for high-stakes meetings that need advance preparation:
- Customer/prospect executive meetings
- Board / investor meetings
- Analyst or press briefings
- Travel or events

For each, flag:
- How far out the meeting is
- Whether prep has started (materials shared, prep session scheduled)
- Who's driving prep so {{USER_NAME}} knows who to nudge

### 6. One Thing to Watch
- The single most important thing to be aware of for tomorrow

## Format

Short narrative style with context. Not a wall of bullets — tell the story of what tomorrow looks like and what needs attention. Keep it to 1-2 screens unless the day is unusually heavy.
