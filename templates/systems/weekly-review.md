# Weekly Review System

> **Depends on:** `systems/email-triage.md` (search methodology, priority tiers)

The weekly review bridges weeks — close out what happened, set up what's coming.

## When

Sunday evening. {{USER_NAME}} reads it to get their head right for Monday.

## Review Sections

### 1. Past Week Recap
What happened this week that mattered. Short narrative, not a log of every meeting.
- Key wins — deals closed, milestones hit, good meetings
- Misses or setbacks — anything that didn't go as planned
- Surprises — things that came out of nowhere, good or bad
- Important decisions made (and by whom)

### 3. Message Cleanup

**Emails:**
- Tier 1 or 2 emails from the past week that are still unresolved
- Threads where someone is waiting on {{USER_NAME}}
- Emails {{USER_NAME}} said they'd follow up on but haven't

**Messages (Teams/Slack):**
- Unresolved DMs or priority group chats
- Label each as [DM] or [Group: name]

Always keep email and messaging in separate sections.

### 4. Next Week Preview
- Full calendar overview for the upcoming week
- Flag high-stakes meetings that need advance prep
- Note office days vs. remote days
- Any travel or logistics to be aware of
- Conflicts or scheduling crunches

### 5. Priorities Check
Review `active/priorities.md` against what actually happened:
- Are the current priorities still the right ones?
- Anything to add or drop based on this week's events?
- Suggest updates if needed

### 6. Projects Update
Check in on `active/projects.md`:
- Any movement this week on active projects?
- Projects that have gone quiet — flag stalled ones and suggest who to ping
- Anything discussed in meetings that should update a project entry

### 7. Personal Check
- Family commitments for next week
- Any personal to-dos deferred from this week

## Output Format

Narrative style — a few sentences per section, like a memo from a trusted advisor. Not a wall of checkboxes. Tone should be honest and direct.

Keep it readable in 5-10 minutes. Details available on request for any section.
